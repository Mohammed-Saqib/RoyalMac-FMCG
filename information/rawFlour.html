The Dangers of Eating Flour Raw

While the CDC recommends that you cook the flour to a safe temperature before consuming it, you should also store it separately from other raw foods. It is because flour spreads quickly and can contaminate other foods. Using this precaution is a good idea, especially for young children. Below is a list of signs you may experience when eating flour raw. Hopefully, this information will help you make a more informed decision about baking with flour.

A common ingredient in most recipes, flour is a necessary ingredient to make bread, pastries, and sauces. While flour has many valuable uses, it can also pose serious health risks. Flour includes gluten, a protein located in wheat and other grains. Gluten is responsible for giving bread its elasticity and allowing it to hold together. Ingested gluten can cause symptoms of celiac disease, which affects about one percent of the population. Symptoms include skin rashes and abdominal pain.

E. coli outbreaks involving raw flour are rare. The CDC has advised against eating it because of the health risks of the bacteria. However, in one episode, 63 people contracted E. coli. Luckily, there were no deaths. However, another outbreak in 2021 is linked to cake mix. While it isn't as harmful as eating raw flour, this method is not for everyone. Another common risk associated with raw flour is that it may contain pathogens. Heat-treated flour, which is a safe alternative to refined flour, is best for people who are sensitive to bacteria or allergies. The process of heat-treatment kills pathogens and makes flour safe for baking. However, it is essential to note that the heating process in a microwave will differ from one another. So, make sure you know the timeframes for cooking and baking flour before consuming it.

Before baking with raw flour:

Make sure you wash your hands thoroughly. The sandpaper or countertops you use to prepare your baked goods should be thoroughly cleaned to prevent the spread of bacteria.

Keep a separate bowl for raw dough.

If you're planning to bake with plain flour, don't use it for milkshakes, homemade ice cream, or anything else that requires raw flour. You should also check the ingredients before using them.

Try buckwheat flour if you're looking for a healthy alternative to refined flour. Buckwheat flour is made from grain-like seeds. The taste is strong, but you should be able to find it in a specialty store. If you're not sure which flour to choose, try hazelnut flour. Alternatively, you can try cashew flour, which is tasty and healthy. You can also use almond meals in recipes that call for almond flour.
