Fruits

The genus and species of fruit depend on the way they are formed. Plant scientists classify fruits into three main groups: simple fruits, aggregate fruits, and multiple fruits. These groupings are determined by the arrangement of ovary cells and how the fruit develops. In addition, some plants belong to various groups and possess multiple subtypes. Fungi are also members of the plant kingdom. A series of transformations characterize their fruiting bodies during the development of a fertilized carpel.
